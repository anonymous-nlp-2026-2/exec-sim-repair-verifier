\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented the first systematic comparison of three execution-free verification formulations (\cls{}, \cwm{}, \traj{}) under ${\leq}$8B LoRA fine-tuning. At function level, \cls{} and \cwm{} are statistically equivalent (TOST $p{=}0.007$, 5 seeds); \traj{} equivalence is inconclusive after Holm correction. At repository level, divergence reaches 18--26\pp{} ($z{=}-23.7$), consistent across families (\S\ref{sec:threats}). Preliminary 14B results (3 seeds, single family) narrow all gaps to within 1.07\pp{}; whether the repo-level divergence is formulation-inherent or capacity-contingent remains open. Future directions include complexity-aware routing, scaling to 32B+~\cite{meta2025cwm}, and per-test diagnostics for downstream repair.

\section*{Limitations}

Primary findings use ${\leq}$8B LoRA fine-tuning with multi-seed replication; 14B results use a single model family (Qwen3) with 3 seeds; cross-family replication and evaluation at 32B+ are needed. The repo-level gap may diminish at 32B+ scale or with execution-trace pretraining~\cite{meta2025cwm}. All evaluation uses Python-only benchmarks; other languages may exhibit different format compliance rates. Training data uses synthetic mutation operators~\cite{andrews2005mutation,just2014mutants}; the synthetic-to-real transfer gap remains untested (Appendix~\ref{app:limitations}). Format-specific interventions (constrained decoding, output-format pretraining) may mitigate \cwm{}'s format collapse but were not evaluated.
