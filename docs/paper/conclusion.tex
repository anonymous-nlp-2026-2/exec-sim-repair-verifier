\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented the first systematic comparison of three execution-free verification formulations (\cls{}, \cwm{}, \traj{}). At function level, \cls{} and \cwm{} are statistically equivalent (TOST $p{=}0.007$, 5 seeds); \traj{} reaches parity on Qwen3 but not DeepSeek-Coder (2.69\pp{}). Per-test classification achieves 217$\times$ throughput while providing unconfirmed diagnostic signal. Complementary errors (oracle: 90.63\%/95.83\%) motivate complexity-aware routing. At repository level under ${\leq}$8B LoRA fine-tuning, a robust interaction ($z{=}-23.7$) confirms divergence (19--26\pp{}, single-seed variant-level), with \cwm{} falling below majority baseline---consistent across families but dependent on multiple confounds (\S\ref{sec:threats}). \cls{} is recommended for throughput; behavior at larger scale remains open~\cite{meta2025cwm}.

\section*{Limitations}

Most repo-level comparisons use a single seed; the gap magnitude (19--26\pp{}) exceeds any single assessed confound (\S\ref{sec:threats}), but correlated confounds and limited seeds mean definitive causal attribution requires multi-seed replication. The two-seed DeepSeek comparison is exploratory; seed-level power is insufficient for independent confirmation of the Qwen3 finding. Our \cwm{} baseline uses ${\leq}$8B LoRA fine-tuning, not the original 32B CWM with execution trace mid-training~\cite{meta2025cwm}; at larger scale, the generation bottleneck may diminish. However, matched comparison at controlled capacity isolates the formulation effect from scale confounds---complementary to work scaling execution simulators. Training data comprises synthetic mutations~\cite{andrews2005mutation,just2014mutants}; the synthetic-to-real transfer gap for absolute accuracy remains untested, though the coupling effect supports relative ranking validity: our core finding---that formulation choice matters at repo level but not function level---concerns relative ordering, expected to be preserved under distributional shifts that affect all formulations equally (Appendix~\ref{app:limitations}). \traj{} training uses variant-level labels (386 instances vs.\ ${\sim}$70K for \cls{}/\cwm{}), which limits direct comparison. A re-repair pilot ($n{=}44$, $p{=}0.42$; Appendix~\ref{app:extended_analysis}(j)) found no benefit from per-test diagnostics. The mixed-effects regressions use 25--139 problem-level clusters (cross-model and cross-complexity respectively); fixed-effect estimates pool across $>$56K observations, but the random-effects structure has limited resolution for the 25-cluster model. Model coverage is limited to 6--8B code-specialized LLMs; generalization to larger or general-purpose models is untested. Extended discussion: Appendix~\ref{app:limitations}. All code, data, and model checkpoints will be released upon acceptance.
